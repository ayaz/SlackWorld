Issue #1, April 2005

Editorial

Ladies and gentlemen, folks, fellow slackers,

This is the first issue of The Slack World, another project dedicated to the best Linux distribution in the observable part of the Universe, namely, Slackware. ;-) The project was initially planned as a monthly newsletter/magazine aimed to fill a few gaps that we see in covering Slackware-related information by other resources, by providing in-depth articles, and a summary of recent Slackware-related news.

This first issue contains some sections that we would like to become traditional:

  • Editorial: what is a magazine without an editorial? The editorial will comment on new developments in the GNU/Linux world, or beg for new articles.
  • Slack News: the Slack News section is a summary of recent developments related to Slackware Linux, mainly to its -current branch.
  • Slackopedia: the Slackopedia section contains an in-depth article about some Slackware-related subject.
  • Slack Wisdom: Slack Wisdom is a section that gathers Slack wisdom from various newsgroups and mailing lists.

We hope you will enjoy this first issue, have a good read! Your feedback is welcome.


Editors of Issue #1,

Daniel de Kok and Mikhail Zotov

P.S. Unfortunately, Daniel de Kok had to leave the project due to other duties when the issue was nearly ready. I will not be able to maintain a monthly magazine/newsletter alone. Thus I invite experienced Slackers who like the idea of the project to share their knowledge with the rest of the community by submitting articles. Besides this, I invite anybody who would like to make The Slack World appear on a regular basis to join the project as an editor. It is a big work but also great fun! Equal rights are guaranteed. :-) -- MZ, user e-boogie registered at yandex.ru
